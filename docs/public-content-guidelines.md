## Public Content Guidelines

### 1. Purpose

Public pages must be written for real users. They should not be written as notes to AI tools, maintainers, or reviewers.

### 2. What Belongs in Public Pages

- User rights and obligations
- Privacy notices
- Security and risk notices
- Complaint, reporting, and appeal paths
- Campus credential consent and credential-control explanations
- Non-official-service disclaimers
- Trademark, license, and open-source boundary explanations

### 3. What Must Not Appear in Public Pages

- AI, Codex, or ChatGPT drafting notes
- TODO items
- Internal implementation instructions
- “Write this if...” or placeholder drafting notes
- Unverified legal-subject placeholders
- Unverified contact, address, or filing information
- Excessive framework, template, or starter-documentation links

### 4. README Style

- Keep the README concise.
- Do not duplicate full policy text in the README.
- Do not list excessive user-policy hyperlinks if the app already has an About or Policy entry.
- README may link to developer governance documents where useful.

### 5. Policy Date Handling

- Do not change policy dates for typo fixes, editorial cleanup, or style updates.
- Only update dates when there is a substantive change to user rights, obligations, privacy processing, dispute handling, or service rules.

### 6. Compliance Wording

- Use accurate and cautious wording.
- Do not describe the project as an official school service, school-authorized service, or school-endorsed service unless verified.
- Do not invent legal entities, addresses, emails, filing numbers, or contact details.

### 7. Review Checklist

- No internal notes
- No AI-dialogue text
- No unverified legal information
- No unnecessary framework or template links
- Necessary user risk notices remain visible
- No policy dates changed without a substantive reason
